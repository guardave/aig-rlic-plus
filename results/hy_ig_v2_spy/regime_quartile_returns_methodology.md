# Regime Quartile Returns — Methodology

**Pair:** `hy_ig_v2_spy`
**Artifact:** `regime_quartile_returns.csv`
**Rule:** ECON-E2 (regime quartile return artifact persistence)
**Stakeholder item addressed:** S18-8 (restores "Annualised SPX return by quartile Q1–Q4" that was silently dropped from the CCF Evidence page in the v2 rerun).

## Regime-definition rule

- **Observation unit:** calendar month-end (monthly resampling from the daily master dataset `data/hy_ig_v2_spy_daily_20260410.parquet`).
- **Regime variable:** `hy_ig_spread` level (high-yield minus investment-grade OAS, in percentage points), observed at month-end.
- **Bucketing:** unconditional empirical quartiles over the full monthly sample (2000-02-29 to 2025-12-31, N = 311 months). `Q1` = lowest-spread quartile (tightest credit conditions), `Q4` = highest-spread quartile (widest / most stressed credit conditions).
- **Cutoffs (empirical):**
  - min = 1.520 pp
  - Q1 / Q2 = 2.575 pp
  - Q2 / Q3 = 3.240 pp
  - Q3 / Q4 = 4.620 pp
  - max = 13.360 pp

## SPY metric computation per quartile

For the subset of months assigned to each quartile:

- `ret_m` = monthly SPY log return (`log(spy_t / spy_{t-1})`) computed at month-end from the master dataset.
- `ann_return` = mean(`ret_m`) × 12
- `ann_vol` = stdev(`ret_m`, ddof=1) × sqrt(12)
- `sharpe` = `ann_return` / `ann_vol` (zero risk-free rate; conditional, not a tradable strategy Sharpe)
- `max_drawdown` = maximum peak-to-trough drawdown of the conditional cumulative equity curve formed by sequencing the quartile's monthly returns in date order and compounding. This is a conditional drawdown, not a contiguous time-period drawdown.
- `n_months` = number of monthly observations in the quartile.

## Interpretation notes

- The conditional drawdown metric mixes months that are non-contiguous in real time. It is meaningful as a regime-conditional severity statistic, not as a tradable strategy metric. Stakeholders should interpret it as "when we are in this spread regime, how deep has the worst cumulative drawdown been across those episodes?"
- The Sharpe values shown are conditional-mean Sharpe ratios, not out-of-sample strategy Sharpe ratios. They quantify the ex-post reward-per-unit-risk of being long SPY during each spread regime, not the Sharpe of a signal-conditioned strategy.
- The monotonic Q1 → Q4 gradient in ann_return (positive in Q1/Q2, negative in Q4) is the headline stakeholder finding and the content of the CCF Evidence bar chart that was silently dropped.
