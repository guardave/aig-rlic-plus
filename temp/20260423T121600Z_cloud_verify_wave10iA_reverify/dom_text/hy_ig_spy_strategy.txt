The Strategy: Translating Credit Signals into SPY Positioning

We tested hundreds of strategy combinations to find the most robust way to time SPY exposure using the HY-IG credit spread signal.

Tournament Winner: S6_hmm_stress / P2_signal_strength / LN/A

Strategy Rule in Plain English: Monitor the HMM stress probability derived from the HY-IG credit spread. When the stress probability exceeds the 0.5 threshold (credit market signaling stress regime), scale down SPY exposure proportionally to the probability level. When stress probability falls below 0.5 (calm regime), restore full SPY exposure. The HMM is updated monthly using available spread history.

How the Signal is Generated

Each day, the HY-IG spread (difference between ICE BofA HY OAS and IG OAS) is computed from FRED series BAMLH0A0HYM2EY and BAMLC0A0CMEY. A 2-state Hidden Markov Model (HMM) fitted to the daily spread history produces a continuous probability of being in the 'stress' vs 'calm' state. This probability (hmm_2state_prob_stress) is the tournament-winning signal (S6_hmm_stress). The signal operates at L0 — no additional lead lag beyond the HMM's own probabilistic smoothing. Monthly portfolio positions are rebalanced at each month-end based on the end-of-month stress probability.

How to Use the Signal — Trigger Scenarios

What this shows: Strategy family P2 (Signal Strength) · threshold loaded from winner_summary.json = 0.5 · direction: countercyclical.

REDUCE

When HMM Stress Probability rises above 0.5 → scale SPY exposure down proportionally. At signal = 1.0, exposure = 0%.

HOLD

When HMM Stress Probability lingers near 0.5 → hold exposure at roughly 50%. The signal-strength rule prevents whipsaw.

BUY

When HMM Stress Probability falls below 0.5 → scale SPY exposure back up. At signal = 0, exposure = 100%.

How to read it: sparklines are drawn from real history (signals_20260422.parquet column hmm_2state_prob_stress) — 2/3 scenarios located; thresholds match the runtime rule. The dashed horizontal line is the decision threshold at which the action in each card fires.

How to Use This Indicator Manually

If you want to use this signal with minimal code:

Pull HY and IG OAS from FRED (BAMLH0A0HYM2EY and BAMLC0A0CMEY) at month-end.
Compute the spread: HY OAS minus IG OAS in percentage points.
Run a 2-state HMM on the full spread history using hmmlearn (or similar). The higher-mean state is 'stress'; the lower-mean state is 'calm'.
Read the current stress probability from the final observation.
Scale SPY position: full weight when probability < 0.5; scaled proportionally when probability ≥ 0.5. The model re-estimates monthly.
Re-estimate monthly — HMM parameters should be refreshed annually or after major structural breaks.
