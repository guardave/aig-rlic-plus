The Strategy: Translating DFF-TED Signals into SPY Positioning

We tested ~991 strategy combinations on DFF - DTB3 (Variant B) — the longest-history TED variant — to find the most robust timing rule.

How the Signal is Generated
Compute the daily DFF-TED spread (DFF − DTB3).
Compute its 21-day rate of change: roc_21 = spread_t / spread_{t-21} − 1.
Fix the 25th-percentile threshold on the in-sample window (1993-01 to 2017-12).
Each day, map the gap between today's roc_21 and the threshold to a proportional position size in [0, 100%] SPY exposure.
Apply a 5-day lead lag — today's signal drives positioning five business days hence.

How to Use the Signal — Trigger Scenarios

Trigger thresholds shown use a default heuristic (0.5) — this pair's winner_summary.json.threshold_value is on a legacy non-numeric schema and could not be coerced to a float. Numeric trigger cards will display after the pair pipeline is rerun against the current schema.

What this shows: Strategy family P2 (Signal Strength (Proportional)) · threshold loaded from winner_summary.json = 0.5 · direction: countercyclical.

REDUCE

When Spread 21-Day ROC rises above 0.5 → scale SPY exposure down proportionally. At signal = 1.0, exposure = 0%.

HOLD

When Spread 21-Day ROC lingers near 0.5 → hold exposure at roughly 50%. The signal-strength rule prevents whipsaw.

BUY

When Spread 21-Day ROC falls below 0.5 → scale SPY exposure back up. At signal = 0, exposure = 100%.

How to Use This Indicator Manually

A human operator replicating the rule would:

Pull daily DFF and DTB3 from FRED.
Maintain a rolling 21-day RoC series.
Compare today's RoC to the fixed 25th-percentile benchmark (in the methodology appendix).
Scale SPY position size proportionally to the gap below the threshold; exit to cash when the signal is above.
Review weekly — annual turnover is low (~10 trades), so weekly monitoring is sufficient.
