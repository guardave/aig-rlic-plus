The Strategy: Translating Spliced-TED Signals into SPY Positioning

We tested ~991 strategy combinations on the Spliced-TED series (Variant C) to find the most robust extended-history timing rule.

How the Signal is Generated
Construct the spliced daily series: TEDRATE for 1993-01 through 2022-01, then adjusted_DFF_TED = DFF_TED × scale + shift (calibrated on overlap) from 2022-02 onwards.
Compute its 21-day rate of change.
Fix the 25th-percentile threshold on the in-sample window (1993-01 to 2017-12).
Map the gap between today's RoC and the threshold to a proportional SPY exposure in [0, 100%].
Apply a 1-day lead lag — today's signal implements tomorrow's position.

How to Use the Signal — Trigger Scenarios

Trigger thresholds shown use a default heuristic (0.5) — this pair's winner_summary.json.threshold_value is on a legacy non-numeric schema and could not be coerced to a float. Numeric trigger cards will display after the pair pipeline is rerun against the current schema.

What this shows: Strategy family P2 (Signal Strength (Proportional)) · threshold loaded from winner_summary.json = 0.5 · direction: countercyclical.

REDUCE

When Spread 21-Day ROC rises above 0.5 → scale SPY exposure down proportionally. At signal = 1.0, exposure = 0%.

HOLD

When Spread 21-Day ROC lingers near 0.5 → hold exposure at roughly 50%. The signal-strength rule prevents whipsaw.

BUY

When Spread 21-Day ROC falls below 0.5 → scale SPY exposure back up. At signal = 0, exposure = 100%.

How to read it: sparklines are drawn from stylised (no signals parquet found); thresholds match the runtime rule. The dashed horizontal line is the decision threshold at which the action in each card fires.

How to Use This Indicator Manually

A human operator replicating the rule would:

Pull the latest TEDRATE (through 2022) and DFF, DTB3 (through today) from FRED.
Apply the published affine adjustment to extend TEDRATE post-2022.
Maintain a rolling 21-day RoC series.
Compare today's RoC to the fixed 25th-percentile benchmark; scale SPY position proportionally when below.
Rebalance next business day (1-day lead). Monthly review is sufficient given ~14 round-trips per year.
