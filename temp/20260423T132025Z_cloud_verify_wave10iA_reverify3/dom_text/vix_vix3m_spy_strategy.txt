The Strategy: Translating VIX Term-Structure Signals into SPY Positioning

We tested hundreds of strategy combinations to find the most robust way to time SPY exposure using the VIX/VIX3M ratio.

Tournament Winner: S3_z126 / P1_long_cash / LN/A

Tournament winner: Signal S3_z126 (126-day z-score of the VIX/VIX3M ratio) / Threshold T2_rp75 (rolling 75th percentile) / Strategy P1_long_cash / Lead 0 (same-day).

Compute the VIX/VIX3M ratio's z-score over a trailing 126-day (6-month) window. When this z-score is below its rolling 75th percentile — meaning term-structure stress is not extreme — stay long SPY. When the z-score exceeds the 75th percentile (acute backwardation / panic), move to cash. No leverage, no shorting.

How the Signal is Generated
Pull VIX and VIX3M. Yahoo Finance tickers ^VIX and ^VIX3M. Daily.
Compute the ratio. ratio_t = VIX_t / VIX3M_t.
Compute the 126-day z-score. Rolling 126-day mean and standard deviation of the ratio; z_t = (ratio_t - mean) / std.
Compute the rolling 75th-percentile threshold. Rolling quantile of the z-score over the same 126-day window.
Translate to position. If z_t < threshold → long SPY (+1). Otherwise → cash (0). Rebalance at the next market open.

How to Use This Indicator Manually

This is a daily-cadence rule, so it is most practical with a simple spreadsheet or a five-line Python script rather than eyeball calculation. Pipeline-reproducible steps:

Daily download of ^VIX and ^VIX3M closes from Yahoo.
Keep the last 126 business days in a ring buffer.
Compute ratio, z-score, and rolling 75th-percentile threshold.
Rebalance SPY position at next open based on today's close.

Turnover is ~23 round-trips per year — meaningful but well inside daily-rebalance execution budgets.
